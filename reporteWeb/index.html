What is Chess 24?

  • Chess 24 is a mobile chess game. Players can play as guest of create an account to enjoy different types of chess games.
  • Play with another human or vs a computer.
  • Watch professional tournaments.
  • Review and analyze plays and figure out the best choice to make.
  • Watch free and paid videos on how to play.

Google play information

  • It has over 100.000 downloads
  • Average score is 4.2 with over 5.000 reviews
  • Not open source.

Procedure applied

  • Due to the app not being open source, we decompiled the apk file so we could check the code
  • We changed the manifest to the app was debuggable and we could measure performance with android profiler
  • That way, we played around with the app. And for every interesting thing that we saw, we expected the code to explained it
  • We also reviewed the code in order to understand the architecture and other design desitions made

Motivation

During daily use of the application we found that when rotate the phone multiple times on an offline match the app will generate multiple empty dialogs, this behaviour let us hypothesize that the app will contian several errors including memory leaks, view leaks and others.

Other users opinions

Using sentiment analysis in the reviews of the app we discover that more thah half of the users has a good opinion of the app, also reading the reviews we observe that the most common problem reported is the lack of eventual conectivity in critical parts of the app .

General architecture

  • The overall app is composed of 4 Main packages, 2 custom and 2 derived from libraries.
  • The first package is in charge of storing the Activities, Fragments, animations and the drawing of the board
  • The second package is in charge of handling the login of a match and other util classes like an Asynchronous custom HTTP request class.
  • The third package is a socketio library, in charge of handling the online matches and warn the user if it lost the connection during a match.
  • Finally, the fourth package is in charge of handling a Service for the login behind the matches. That is, when the user choose to play a match, the app launches a Service to handle in background all heavy tasks, mainly related to the logic of the game. This library is based on a popular OS library and it is made in C++, therefore is beyond the reach of this report.

Most critical TO-DOs

  • Invest heavily in eventual connectivity

  • Allow multiple users to store their plays (and don't share them)

  • Watch out for screen rotations
